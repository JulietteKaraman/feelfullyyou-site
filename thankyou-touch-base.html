Touch Base™

A tiny gesture. A massive shift.

This short video introduces you to the Touch Base™ cue — a discreet, powerful physical anchor that signals to your body:

"I'm here. I'm safe. I can stay."

It's the foundational cue I teach all my private clients — including those navigating phobias, trauma, sudden shutdowns, or a tendency to leave their body the moment touch feels like too much.

Because this isn't just about touch. It's about helping your system feel safe enough to receive it.

  • A simple technique to practise before offering or receiving touch — especially when things feel rushed, disconnected, or performative
  • Anchors your breath, your body, and your attention — without needing to say a word
  • Creates a full-body "pause" moment, where safety can override survival
  • So discreet you can use it in bed, at dinner, mid-argument, or before intimacy

Why it works

Touch Base™ activates pressure points that — when paired with slowness, breath, and focus — downregulate the stress response.

You're gently retraining your system to signal: "There's no tiger. I don't have to brace. I'm safe to feel this."

And here's the real magic: it might feel silly at first (totally normal). But this soft, SLOOOOOOOW gesture becomes a kind of invisible safety blanket. Your partner won't even notice.

But your body will.

What's really happening under the hood

Your body is always scanning. Especially if you're a woman — or were socialised as one — your subconscious is constantly asking:

"Is it safe to soften here?"   "Is it safe to let go?"

All it takes is one loaded moment — a look. A brush. A hand that lingered where it shouldn't have — and your body registers it. You might not even remember when. But your system does.

It braces.

And often — even in loving relationships — this is why touch doesn't land. Not because you don't want to receive it. But because your body's still armoured, just in case.

This gesture interrupts that loop — without drama. No need for emotional excavation. No need to explain yourself mid-connection.

The practice

Touch Base™

  • 1Press your thumb gently to your forefinger.
  • 2Continue rubbing. SLOOOOOW — soft like melted chocolate. Slower than that. Even slower than you think slow is.
  • 3Say to yourself, quietly or silently: I am safe right now.
  • 4Keep rubbing for the full two minutes. Breathe.

The whole practice is two minutes. That's it. This is where the shift begins.
