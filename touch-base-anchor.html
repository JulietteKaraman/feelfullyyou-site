The Touch Base Anchor

Two minutes. No equipment. A way back to yourself, anywhere, any time.

This is for someone who loves the person in front of them and cannot find the door back in.

Most of us have learned to live above the neck. We think, we plan, we worry. We forget that the body is there, holding all of it.

The Touch Base Anchor is a practice I have used for years, with clients and on myself. It does not require anything from you. It only asks you to slow down for two minutes and feel.

That is enough to shift the nervous system. To move from scattered to present. From braced to open.

You were told The Spontaneity Lie: that real closeness arrives on its own, and anything built on purpose is not the real thing. It does not arrive on its own. It is built.
