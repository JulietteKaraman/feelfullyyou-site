Why me

I'm Juliette.

I've spent over two decades in the room with men and couples at exactly this impasse, men who are trying, who love the women in their lives, who can't understand why trying isn't enough.

But before I was the practitioner in that room, I was the woman on the other side of it. I know what it feels like to stiffen when the man you love reaches for you. To pull away and have no language for why. To perform desire because the real thing felt too far to access.

It took me decades to understand what I was braced against, what I needed and had never been given, and what structure would have changed everything. So I followed my own wound into every room it needed to understand.

Fifteen years ago I started noticing something in almost every couple I worked with, the bedroom had gone quiet, but the real disconnection was happening everywhere else. In the kitchen. On the sofa. In the two seconds after one of them reached out and the other pulled back without quite knowing why.

Safe touch outside the bedroom is where intimacy actually lives. I've spent over two decades and thousands of client sessions proving that. When the body learns that reaching is safe, that a hand on the back means nothing is being asked of you, everything else begins to shift.

That's why I built these rituals. Not as a path to sex. As a path back to each other.
